## The Quick Download

The cost of custom software is collapsing. That changes everything — not just how you build, but how you sell, price, and think about competitive moats. Multi-tenant SaaS was a workaround for expensive development. When you can build bespoke in a weekend, the workaround stops making sense.

## What I'm Actually Thinking About

Most of the product and business writing in the AI space is about tools — which model is best, which API to use, how to prompt. That's not what this is. This is about the second-order questions: what happens to your business model when software gets cheap, what it means to build a product on top of infrastructure you don't control, and whether the thing you're building has any defensible value in a world where the underlying capability is commoditizing fast.

## The Stuff That Matters

**Margins and moats.** When OpenAI can replicate your feature in a model update, where does your value actually live? The answer is almost always in distribution, data, or workflow integration — rarely in the model itself.

**The death of one-size-fits-all.** Enterprise software was built around the assumption that customization is expensive. When it isn't, every business can have a bespoke tool built for exactly how they work. What does that do to the Salesforces of the world?

**Selling AI.** The pitch problem is real. Most buyers are either skeptical or have been burned by vendor hype. The companies winning right now are the ones with narrow, specific claims and fast time-to-value demonstrations.
