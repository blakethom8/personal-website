## The Quick Download

The mechanics of building with agents — not the theory, the actual doing. This is where I put the architecture notes, deployment write-ups, and the things I had to figure out the hard way. If you're building something real with AI, these are the pieces that matter.

## What Lives Here

OpenClaw is the main project I'm writing about right now — an agent-native healthcare data platform that I've been building on Azure. Most of the technical writing here comes out of that work directly. When I hit something interesting (or frustrating), I write it up.

The topics that keep coming up: how to structure multi-agent pipelines, what Azure Container Apps actually costs at scale, the gap between what the RAG tutorials show you and what you actually need to build, and how to think about data pipelines when your inputs are messy claims data.

## The Pattern

I learn best by building first and writing second. These posts aren't theoretical. They're explanations of decisions I actually made, tools I actually used, and mistakes I actually made first. The goal is to save someone else the same two days of debugging I just had.

## What's Ahead

More on agent orchestration patterns, infrastructure as code for AI workloads, and the practicalities of working with large healthcare datasets in Azure. Still early on some of this — more coming as OpenClaw develops.
